# Member Feedback Analysis Memo

## Executive summary

Across 500 member feedback entries, the core medical experience is consistently strong, but the surrounding operational journey is fragile. Most members describe the scan itself, the technology, and the clinical team as excellent and often “life‑changing,” yet a meaningful minority report repeated friction with scheduling, digital tools, communication, billing, and result timelines.

In short: the product is strong, but the experience around it is uneven. If Preventative Scan can bring its operational layer up to the level of its clinical offering, it will unlock both higher trust and more durable loyalty.

---

## Dataset overview

- **Total feedback entries:** 500  
- **Categories used:** App Issues, Customer Support, Billing, Lab Delays, Confusion About Results, Other, Positive Feedback  
- **Structure:** Each entry was tagged with a primary category and a severity score from 1 (minor) to 5 (severe).

Examples of the range of feedback include:

- “The scan itself was quick and the technician made me feel comfortable. Results came back faster than expected… Worth every penny.”  
- “Tried to reschedule my scan three times… Still haven't received confirmation… Really frustrated with this process.”  

These illustrate the core pattern: high satisfaction with the scan and clinical team, contrasted with frustration around access, systems, and follow‑through.

---

## Key quantitative patterns

Because the dataset is fully categorized, we can see clear patterns by theme and severity:

- **Positive Feedback** is the single largest category.  
  - Many members describe the experience as “the best healthcare decision I’ve made,” “life‑changing,” or “worth every penny.”  
  - Severity scores here are almost always **1–2**, reflecting delight or minor nitpicks rather than real pain.

- **App Issues** and **Customer Support** together make up a large share of negative experiences.  
  - App Issues entries often carry **severity 3–5**, especially when booking fails, appointments disappear, or the portal blocks access to results.  
  - Customer Support complaints frequently sit at **severity 4–5**, particularly when members cannot get timely responses about results, scheduling, or urgent questions.

- **Billing** and **Lab Delays** are smaller in volume but high in intensity.  
  - Billing issues (unexpected fees, rigid cancellation policies, mismatched quotes) are often **severity 4–5** and described as “nightmare,” “draconian,” or “no humanity in their policies.”  
  - Lab Delays entries cluster around **severity 3–5**, especially when promised timelines (e.g., “told 7–10 days”) stretch into multiple weeks with poor communication.

- **Confusion About Results** appears as a mid‑sized category with mixed severity.  
  - Some members felt rushed in results review or received conflicting information across channels.  
  - Severity tends to sit in the **2–4** range, reflecting anxiety and uncertainty rather than outright system failure.

- **Other** captures environment, amenities, and general “value for price” sentiment.  
  - These are typically **severity 2–3**: not catastrophic, but enough to erode the premium positioning when combined with other issues.

## Thematic insights

### 1. Core clinical value is very strong

Across the Positive Feedback category, several consistent themes emerge:

- Members repeatedly describe the scan as **comprehensive**, **thorough**, and **cutting‑edge**.  
- Many mention that something important was caught early or that they now have a “baseline” and “roadmap” for their health.  
- The radiologists, Scan Advisors, and clinical staff are frequently praised as **knowledgeable**, **caring**, and **professional**.

This creates a strong foundation: the core product is delivering on its promise.

### 2. Digital and scheduling systems are a major friction point

App Issues feedback highlights:

- Booking flows that show availability but fail on confirmation.  
- Crashes or errors when entering payment or insurance information.  
- Portals that are confusing, buggy, or block access to results or paperwork.  
- System errors leading to lost appointments, wrong reminders, or last‑minute changes.

These issues are often described as “a disaster,” “embarrassing for a tech‑forward company,” or “a nightmare,” and they frequently carry severity 4–5 because they waste time, create anxiety, or cause missed appointments.

### 3. Communication and follow‑up are inconsistent and often too slow

Customer Support and Lab Delays together paint a picture of **slow, fragmented, and impersonal communication**:

- Members report waiting days to weeks for responses to messages about results, billing, or scheduling.  
- Escalations often require multiple calls, voicemails, or portal messages.  
- When results are delayed beyond the promised window, updates are vague or absent.

This is especially problematic when the topic is **abnormal or concerning findings**—members explicitly say delays here feel “unacceptable” given the stakes.

### 4. Policies and billing feel misaligned with the “concierge” promise

Billing feedback shows:

- Surprise fees for reschedules or cancellations even when notice exceeds stated policy.  
- Rigid enforcement of policies in the context of emergencies or public health concerns.  
- Charges that don’t match initial quotes, with slow or unclear resolution.

Members use language like “draconian,” “no humanity,” and “not worth the premium price,” which directly undermines the brand’s positioning as high‑touch, preventive, and member‑centric.

### 5. Expectations vs reality gap on “premium” and “concierge”

Across Other, Customer Support, and App Issues, there’s a recurring theme:

- Marketing and pricing set an expectation of **concierge‑level, seamless care**.  
- Operational reality often feels **standard or below‑average**, with multiple small friction points (parking, directions, cold facilities, impersonal interactions, confusing prep instructions).

Members explicitly call out this gap: “Expected concierge service based on marketing. Got standard healthcare experience… Not worth the premium price.”

---

## Recommendations

Based on the patterns above, the highest‑leverage improvements are operational, not clinical.

### 1. Stabilize and simplify digital booking and portal flows

- **Prioritize reliability over new features** in the booking and portal experience.  
- Address known issues where availability appears but fails on confirmation, and where documents or results fail to load.  
- Add clear, inline guidance for insurance, payment, and prep requirements to reduce confusion and support tickets.

Success metric: reduction in App Issues complaints and fewer high‑severity (4–5) entries tied to booking and portal problems.

### 2. Redesign communication SLAs for results and support

- Establish and publish **clear response time standards** for messages about results, billing, and scheduling.  
- Implement internal routing and ownership so urgent clinical questions are triaged and answered quickly.  
- Proactively communicate when results will be delayed beyond the promised window, with specific new timelines.

Success metric: fewer complaints about “no response,” “had to follow up multiple times,” and “terrible communication,” especially in Customer Support and Lab Delays.

### 3. Align billing policies with preventive, member‑centric positioning

- Review cancellation and reschedule policies for flexibility in cases of emergencies, illness, or public health concerns.  
- Ensure quotes and final charges match, with clear pre‑scan explanations of what is and isn’t included.  
- Empower frontline staff to resolve obvious mismatches quickly instead of bouncing members between departments.

Success metric: reduction in high‑severity Billing complaints and fewer mentions of “draconian,” “nightmare,” or “no humanity.”

### 4. Standardize prep, expectations, and results communication

- Create a single source of truth for prep instructions and ensure website, email, and phone guidance match.  
- Provide a simple “What to Expect” overview before the scan, including duration, fasting requirements, and follow‑up timelines.  
- For results, ensure members have enough time and space to ask questions, especially when findings are abnormal.

Success metric: fewer Confusion About Results entries and fewer mentions of conflicting information or rushed reviews.

### 5. Protect and amplify the clinical strengths

- Continue investing in radiologist and Scan Advisor time with members—this is a clear differentiator.  
- Capture and reuse positive language from feedback (e.g., “life‑changing,” “roadmap for my health,” “best healthcare decision I’ve made”) in marketing and onboarding.  
- Consider lightweight follow‑up touchpoints (e.g., check‑ins after significant findings) to reinforce the sense of partnership.

Success metric: sustained or increased volume of Positive Feedback with low severity and strong advocacy language.

---

## Conclusion

The feedback makes one thing very clear: Preventative Scan’s **core medical product is excellent**, and members deeply value the insights and early detection it provides. The biggest risks to trust and loyalty are not clinical—they are operational.

By focusing on stabilizing digital systems, tightening communication, humanizing billing, and aligning expectations with reality, Preventative Scan can bring the overall experience up to the level of its clinical excellence and fully deliver on its preventive, premium promise.
